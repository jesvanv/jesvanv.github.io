Elements in groups 3-12 are called the transition elements. The inner transition elements are called the lanthanides and actinides, and are usually put below to fit on a page.

Nonmetals are poor conductors of heat and electricity. Solid nonmetals are brittle and appear dull. With the exception of Hydrogen, the nonmetals are on the right side of the periodic table.

Most of the elements on the periodic table are metals. A metal is typically shiny, opaque and a good conductor of heat and electricty as a pure element. Metals are also malleable, which means they can be bent into different shapes without breaking.

The elements on the border between metals and nonmetals are called metalloids. Silicon is an example of a metalloid element, with properties in-between those of metals and nonmetals.

The main group elements are groups 1-2 and 13-18.
