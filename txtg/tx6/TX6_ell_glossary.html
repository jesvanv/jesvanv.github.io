A

cause (causar) (v) - to make something happen

ce•ment (cementar) (v) - to bind together

change (n) - the act of becoming different

chem•i•cal (químico/a) (adj) - related to chemistry and how matter interacts

com•mon (común) (adj) - happening often; frequent

com•mu•ni•cate (comunicar) (v) - to tell or show

com•pose (componer) (v) - to make up

con•cept (concepto) (n) - an idea

con•clu•sion (conclusión) (n) - a judgment or decision reached by using reason

con•duct (conducir) (v) - to plan and carry out; also to transfer or carry heat or electricity

con•firm (confirmar) (v) - to prove that something is true or correct

con•ser•va•tion (conservación) (n) - the act of saving, protecting, or restoring

con•tent (contenido) (n) - the ideas; the subject matter

cool•er (adj) - at a lower temperature

cre•ate (crear) (v) - to make something

cube (cubo) (n) - something shaped like a block with six equal square sides

cur•rently (adj) (corriente) - right now

cycle (ciclo) (n) - a set of events that are repeated

 

D

dam•age (n) - physical harm; destruction

de•pend (depender) (v) - to be controlled by

de•plet•ed (adj) - used up

de•scribe (describir) (v) - to tell about

di•vide (dividir) (v) - to separate into parts

draw•ing (n) - a picture or diagram

ex•per•i•ment (experimento) (n) - something scientists do to test a hypothesis

ex•plain (explicar) (v) - to make an idea clear by giving details or facts

ex•pla•na•tion (explicación) (n) - a statement that makes something clear; also a reason for an action or a belief

ex•plo•ra•tion (exploración) (n) - the act of learning about something new, sometimes by travel

ex•te•ri•or (exterior) (n) - the outside of something

for•ma•tion (formación) (n) - the act of making something

func•tion (función) (n) - an activity; a task

height (n) - how tall something is

his•to•ry (historia) (n) - the past; something that has already happened

hy•dro•e•lec•tric (hidroeléctrico/a) (adj) - related to generating electricity by using moving water

hy•poth•e•sis (hipótesis) (n) - a possible answer to a scientific question; it must be tested to see if it is correct

length (n) - how long something is

lim•i•ta•tion (limitación) (n) - a flaw or weakness

lo•ca•tion (n) - the place where something is found

move•ment (movimiento) (n) - the act of changing position

or•gan•ism (organismo) (n) - an individual animal, plant, or single-celled life form

par•ti•cle (partícula) (n) - a tiny piece of matter

plan (planear) (v) - to design; to map out

pound (n) - a unit of weight used in the US; 16 ounces

pow•er (v) - to supply with energy

pro•vide (proveer) (v) - to give

R

range (n) - a variety; a selection

reach (v) - to arrive at

rea•son (razón) (n) - a purpose; an intention

re•search (v) - to study something in order to get facts

S
