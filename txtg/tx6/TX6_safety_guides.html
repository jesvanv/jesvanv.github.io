Texas Science Safety Practices and Procedures

PART ONE: Read and follow the Science safety guidelines for each investigation, activity of field experiment.

  1. Listen to all teacher instructions before, during, and after investigations.
  2. Prepare for each investigation or activity.
    1. Sign the Science Safety Student Responsibility Agreement.
    2. Read each activity or investigation carefully.
    3. Identify the investigation purpose.
    4. Work ONLY on activities approved by your teacher.
    5. Follow all oral and written safety instructions.
    6. Know the location of Emergency Safety Equipment such as fire extinguisher, eye and face wash station, safety shower, and first aid kit.
  3. Dress for laboratory work.
    1. Wear protective equipment such as chemical splash goggles, laboratory aprons, and protective gloves as needed.
    2. Roll long sleeves above the wrist.
    3. Tie back long hair.
    4. Remove dangling jewelry and any loose or bulky outer layers of clothing.
    5. Wear shoes that enclose the feet. (no flip flops, sandals or open-toe shoes).
  4. Prevent unsafe situations.
    1. Be aware of classmates and their safety.
    2. Do not touch, taste or smell any substance without teacher instructions.
    3. Never work alone in the laboratory.
    4. Don’t Enter Science or chemical storage or preparatory areas without a teacher.
    5. Keep your work area clean and uncluttered.
  5. Dispose of used or unused materials according to teacher directions.
  6. Return equipment to the proper location.
  7. Wash hands with soap and warm water for 20 seconds after experimenting.
  8. Act wisely in an emergency.
    1. Notify your teacher of any accident immediately.
    2. Follow emergency procedures in event of accident.

PART TWO: Know what to do when...

  1. working with glassware.
    1. Don’t use glassware that is chipped or cracked.
    2. Use special care to prevent breakage and cuts or scratches.
  2. working with heat.
    1. Wear heat-resistant gloves at all times.
    2. Do not touch hot items with bare hands. Use heat resistant gloves, pads or tongs.
    3. Heat water only in open containers of heat-resistant glass.
    4. Watch all burners, hot plates or open flames.
    5. Warn others if they come close to your hot items or liquids.
  3. working with electricity.
    1. Keep electric cords away from water.
    2. Don’t use frayed cords or plugs in outlets.
  4. finished experimenting.
    1. Return clean materials to their proper locations.
    2. Dispose of all used solids and liquids according to teacher instructions. Do not put items in trash or wash down sink without permission.
    3. Wash your hands with soap and water for 20 seconds.
  5. you have safety concerns. Tell your teacher, or get help immediately if:
    1. You have trouble using your equipment.
    2. You do not understand the instructions for the investigation.
    3. You injure yourself, or see someone injured.
    4. You see or smell something burning.
    5. You smell chemical or gas fumes.

Observing safety precautions is an extremely important practice while completing science investigations.

Using science equipment and carrying out laboratory procedures always requires attention to safety. The purpose of learning and discussing safety in the lab is to help you learn how to protect yourself and others at all times.

The investigations in this book are designed to reduce safety concerns in the laboratory. The physics investigations use stable equipment that is easy to operate. The chemistry investigations use both household and laboratory chemicals. Although these chemicals might be familiar to you, they still must be used safely.

You will be introduced to safety by completing a skill sheet to help you observe the safety aids and important information in your science laboratory. In addition to this skill sheet, you may be asked to check your safety understanding and complete a safety contract. Your teacher will decide what is appropriate for your class.

Throughout this book, safety icons and words and phrases like “caution” and “safety tip” are used to highlight important safety information. Read the description for each icon carefully and look out for them when reading your book and doing investigations.

Lab Safety

The Texas Safety Standards are established in the Texas Education code.

For more information or questions about this legislation and details regarding teacher responsibilities, student behavior, hazardous materials, and classroom safety, call your school administration.

TX Safety Standards reference:

Texas Safety Standards, Kindergarten Through Grade 12 Science, Charles A. Dana Center. University of Texas at Austin. 2010.

General safety: Follow all instructions and safety rules to avoid injury to yourself or others.

Wear safety goggles: Requires you to wear protective eyewear such as chemical splash goggles or safety glasses to prevent eye injuries.

Wear a lab apron or coat: Requires you to wear a lab apron or coat to prevent damage to clothing and to protect from possible spills.

Wear gloves: Requires you to protect your hands from injury due to heat or chemicals.

Hazardous chemicals: Requires you to use extreme caution when working with chemicals in the laboratory and to follow all safety and disposal instructions from your teacher.

Skin irritant: Requires you to use extreme caution when handling chemicals in the laboratory due to possible skin irritation and to follow all safety and disposal instructions from your teacher.

Respiratory irritant: Requires you to perform the experiment under a laboratory hood and to avoid inhaling fumes while handling the chemicals.

Laser: Requires you to use extreme caution while using a laser during investigations and to follow all safety instructions.

Sharps: Requires you to use extreme caution when handling sharp objects such as scalpels or glass.

Electrical: Use caution when working with electricity.

TX Safety Standards K-12

TEACHER LABORATORY PRACTICES AND PROCEDURES

(Dana Center, 2010)

  1. Coordinate or participate in a school-level Emergency Response Team.
    1. Inform team of proper procedures for handling chemical burns or fires.
    2. Attend annual professional development to stay current on procedures.
  2. File copies of student safety contracts, which have rules clearly stated.
    1. File student and parent signatures on agreements.
    2. Set clear student expectations for conduct.
    3. Rules are displayed in laboratory.
  3. Maintain a list of known student allergies.
  4. Insure safety equipment is identified, clean, tested regularly and instructions for operation are posted in laboratory area.
    1. Sanitize protective eyewear after each use.
    2. Wash chemical aprons after each spill and at least after each semester.
  5. Maintain a laboratory area that is free of clutter or fire hazard.
    1. Evacuation route posted.
    2. Maintain Materials Safety Data Sheets notebook of Hazardous Chemicals/Biological Wastes
    1. Establish procedures.
    2. Follow disposal guidelines.
  6. Follow routine investigation procedures.
    1. Check compliance with TX Safety Standards.
    2. Check Materials Safety Data Sheets for potential danger and consider substitutes.
    3. Conduct all unfamiliar investigations first.
    4. Check for clarity and concentrations of chemicals.
    5. Anticipate what could go wrong and take appropriate steps.
  7. Guide and monitor students during investigations.
    1. Conduct pre-lab procedure.
    2. Clarify any confusion.
    3. Review safety steps pertaining to procedure.
    4. Review emergency steps as necessary.
    5. Require student compliance with safety equipment.
    6. Maintain discipline.
    7. Correct student non-compliance immediately.
    8. Designate area for chemical retrieval/disposal.
    9. Conduct post-lab cleanup.
  8. Accidents
    1. Follow district first aid procedures.
    2. Write an accident report within one day that contains incident date and time, cause, injury, action taken, and results.

    TX Safety Standards K-12
    TEACHER Field Experience/Activity
    PRACTICES AND PROCEDURES

    (Dana Center, 2010)

    1. ALL Laboratory Safety Rules apply to fieldwork.
    2. Additional Fieldwork considerations and planning include:
      1. Activity has administration approval.
      2. Activity supports Science TEKS, has a clear purpose and is connected to a prior learning experience.
      3. Signed parent permissions obtained.
      4. Medical, or medication, or additional insurance forms obtained.
      5. Additional student supervisors as needed.
      6. Student behavior expectations are clearly communicated.
      7. Contingency plan for inclement weather.
      8. First aid kits readily available.
      9. Cell phone available for emergency calls.

Safety Quiz Answers

  1. Diagrams will vary but should include all safety features and exits.
  2. Answers are:
    1. “Stop, drop, and roll” is for fire blanket.
    2. “Pull, point, and press” is for fire extinguisher
    3. “Face first” is for chemical splash goggles or safety glasses
  3. Answers will vary, but the route your class should take should be posted at each exit.
  4. Answers will vary, but should include: notifying teacher, following oral directions, applying local school procedures, and an explanation of using a piece of safety equipment.
  5. Answers are:
    1. If students accidentally spill a large amount of water on the floor, they should inform teacher first, warn others of spill and get directions on how to clean it up.
    2. If a student knocks over an empty beaker and it breaks on the table, they should inform teacher first, warn others of broken glass and get directions on how to sweep it up and where to find “sharps disposal”.
    3. Students should stay calm, remove goggles and aprons so they don’t trip or fall when exiting, and get oral directions from teacher to evacuate laboratory.
    4. If a lab partner gets some liquid from the experiment in the eye the student should inform the teacher and then proceed to help the student locate the eye and face wash.
    5. If a student smells a “chemical” odor that gives them a headache, they should report it to the teacher and warn others. They should seek a well-ventilated area such as outside, and wait for first aid.

Student Safety Contract

I,                   , (Your name) have read the attached Science Safety Guidelines for Students and understand that all school district rules apply when I am participating in school activities on or off campus. I expect that I may be removed from class and that further disciplinary action may be taken if I do not comply.

  1. I know the purpose and location of:
    • Laboratory apron
    • Protective gloves
    • Eye protection
    • Eye and face wash station
    • Fire alarm, extinguisher and fire blanket
    • First aid kit
  2. I understand the safety information presented.
  3. I will listen to all instructions provided by the teacher.
  4. I will ask questions when I do not understand instructions.
  5. I pledge to follow all of the safety guidelines at all times.
  6. I pledge to be careful about my own safety and to help others be safe.
  7. I am responsible for supporting a safe environment in my classroom and lab.
