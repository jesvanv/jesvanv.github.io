The following is a list of the equipment you may encounter in the science laboratory and how to use it.

Equipment Item

Example

When and How to Use

Give an example of how you would use this equipment in the lab

Anemometers

 

This device measures wind speed or pressure. Velocity, or cup anemometers measure the speed of wind as it hits the cup and turns the blade.

Enter example in your notebook

Balance, triple beam

 

This device measures mass. The number of beams can improve how well the balance can measure. For example, a triple beam balance can measure to the nearest 0.001 grams

Enter example in your notebook

Beaker

 

A beaker is used to hold, mix, or transfer matter. Chemical reactions can also safely take place in beakers made of special glass.

Enter example in your notebook

Calculator

 

A calculator is an electronic device that computes and displays math answers based on keyboard input.

Enter example in your notebook

Collecting net

 

There are different sizes and types of collecting nets. They are used to catch and release live organisms for observation. Most have long handles and are made of fine mesh cloth. Dip nets are used in ponds. Seines are used in shallow marine areas. Butterfly nets have wide circular openings.

Enter example in your notebook

Computer

 

A computer is a programmable, electronic device that can retrieve, edit, process, save and store and send data on demand.

Enter example in your notebook

Cover slip

 

A cover slip is a small square of plastic or glass that is used to cover the specimen on a microscope slide to prevent damage to the microscope lens or loss of the specimen.

Enter example in your notebook

Digital camera

 

A digital camera can record, store and transfer images in a digital format for printing or display on a computer.

Enter example in your notebook

Dropper

 

A dropper transfers liquids in small amounts. About 20 drops of a liquid equals 1 milliliter.

Enter example in your notebook

Electronic balance

 

This device is really not a balance, but a scale. It only measures weight (in newtons, pounds, or ounces and the menu can be set to convert to units of mass). If you take this device to the moon, it will not measure correctly.

Enter example in your notebook

Globe

 

A globe is a sphere-shaped model of the Earth or another solar system object that contains a map on its surface.

Enter example in your notebook

Graduated cylinders (graduate)

 

A graduate is used to measure the volume of a liquid usually in milliliters. The more graduated lines on the cylinder, the better the device measures.

Enter example in your notebook

Hand lens

 

A hand lens is also called a magnifier or magnifying glass. These tools are used to help see more detail for observations. These tools do not work at all to see microscopic objects (see microscopes).

Enter example in your notebook

Hot plate

 

A hot plate uses electricity to heat materials in containers slowly and evenly without open flame.

Enter example in your notebook

Insect trap

 

Insect traps are used to catch insects or monitor where insects can be found. The type of insects they attract depend on their design.

Enter example in your notebook

Metric ruler

 

A metric ruler is used to measure small lengths in centimeters or millimeters.

Enter example in your notebook

Meter stick

 

A meter stick is ued to measure length in metric units.

Enter example in your notebook

Metric tape measure

 

A metric tape measure is used to measure length in metric units.

Enter example in your notebook

Micropipette

 

A micropipette is similar to a dropper, except that it has marks that measure exact volumes.

 

Enter example in your notebook

Microscope slide

 

A microscope slide has items permanently mounted or is plain glass for viewing tiny specimens.

Enter example in your notebook

Microscope

 

A microscope is a device for examining samples too small for the eye to see. It can be adjusted by the operator to change brightness, field, magnification, or focus of a specimen.

Enter example in your notebook

Petri dish

 

A Petri dish, or culture dish is made of glass or plastic. Some dishes are packaged for medical in a clean room to be free of any other particles. Biologists use Petri dishes as temporary places to grow populations of tiny organisms.

Enter example in your notebook

pH probe

 

A pH probe, or sensor is used to measure, on a scale of 1 to 10, the amount of hydrogen ions in a solution. The range is normally from acid (pH = 1) to base
(pH = 10). The device converts the reading into a signal that can be read by an observer or a computer.

Enter example in your notebook

Science journal or notebook

 

Science journals or notebooks have observations, diagrams, results and conclusions typically of experiments but can also be similar to a dated diary. Pages should not be removed and often show corrections over time.

Enter example in your notebook

Psychrometer

 

A psychrometer measures the amount of water vapor (humidity) in the surrounding air. A sling psychrometer uses a thermometer covered with a wet material that is swung in the air for several minutes until it reaches the lowest temperature. The wet and dry bulb temperatures are then compared on a humidity chart.

Enter example in your notebook

Spectroscope

 

A spectroscope is an instrument for observing the various wavelengths of light. Some contain prisms. Others use diffraction gratings (fine slits) to measure various lines of color in nanometers.

Enter example in your notebook

Spring scale

 

A spring scale has a hook on one end to measure the amount of force applied on a coiled spring usually in newtons.

Enter example in your notebook

Stereoscope

 

A stereoscope, or stereomicroscope is a tool used for dissection or in surgery. If stereomicroscope has a two eyepieces that magnify objects exactly as they appear on the stage. The view of an object in a regular microscope is backwards and upside down, so this would be difficult for dissection.

Enter example in your notebook

Temperature probe

 

A temperature probe, or sensor, measures like a thermometer but converts the measurement into a signal that can be read by an observer or computer.

Enter example in your notebook

Test tube

 

Test tubes are used to hold small amounts of material usually undergoing physical changes or chemical reactions.

 

Enter example in your notebook

Test tube rack

 

A test tube rack is a heat resistant or chemical resistant rack in which to place test tubes.

Enter example in your notebook

Thermometer

 

A thermometer measures temperature, the average kinetic energy of substances. The temperature scale will vary, but can measure in degrees Fahrenheit or Celsius.

Enter example in your notebook

Timing devices

 

Timing devices such as clocks, stopwatches, or sensors are used to measure intervals of time. An interval is an amount from a beginning to an end, or from start to finish. Timing devices usually display time in seconds. Some convert seconds to minutes and minutes to hours.

Enter example in your notebook

Water test kit

 

Water test kits contain chemicals in bottles or on test strips. The test kit directions should be followed exactly. Chemicals dropped into the water being tested may react and form new products. A test strip dipped in the water is safer to use, but may not give good results. Any results are compared to a color chart or data table and recorded.

Enter example in your notebook
